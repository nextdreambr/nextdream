# Image Guidelines

Images on the NextDream home must create comfort for people in palliative care,
children in cancer treatment, families, caregivers, supporters, institutions,
and communities.

The goal is presence, dignity, warmth, and trust. Images must never use
suffering as a conversion device.

## Recommended Images

Use photos or visual compositions that communicate:

- Presence.
- Listening.
- Hands held with care.
- Family gathered calmly.
- A child playing, drawing, smiling, or living a light moment.
- An older person being heard with respect.
- Volunteer or supporter in real human interaction.
- Small gestures of care.
- Simple moments of reading, music, conversation, garden, drawing,
  photography, affective food, visit, or company.
- Light, real, welcoming environments.
- Human relationships with respect and consent.

## Images To Avoid

Do not use:

- People crying.
- Hospital beds as the main visual element.
- Children with explicit suffering.
- Images that look like fundraising campaigns.
- Photos that are overly dramatic.
- Dark, cold, or heavy photos.
- Overly artificial stock images.
- People looking down in despair.
- Doctors in authority position as the main image.
- Hospital equipment as the focus.
- Images that expose vulnerability invasively.
- Children in visual context of extreme fragility.
- Images that feel like "antes que seja tarde".
- Images that turn pain into spectacle.

## Aesthetic Direction

Prefer:

- Natural light.
- Warm and soft tones.
- Honest expressions.
- Real environments.
- Clean composition.
- Proximity.
- Low theatricality.
- Low dramatization.
- Humanity before performance.
- Care without sentimental exaggeration.

Avoid:

- Advertising-like scenes.
- Hospital campaign tone.
- Generic stock feeling.
- Overstaged scenes.
- Forced emotional appeal.
- Donation campaign aesthetics.

## Alt Text Rules

Alt text must:

- Describe the visible human interaction respectfully.
- Avoid diagnosing people from the image.
- Avoid exposing vulnerability.
- Avoid phrases that dramatize suffering.
- Be specific enough to support accessibility.

Examples:

- Good: "Pessoa idosa conversando com uma apoiadora em uma mesa com luz natural."
- Good: "Familia reunida em uma conversa tranquila."
- Avoid: "Paciente terminal triste recebendo ajuda."

## Approval Rule

Images involving children, patients, visible illness, family vulnerability,
medical environments, or intimate care require Medical Reviewer approval before
implementation can be considered ready.
